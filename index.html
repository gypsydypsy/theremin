What is a theremin?

The theremin is an electronic musical instrument invented by Leon Theremin in 1928. It has the particularity to be played without any physical contact: its two antennas can sense the position of the player's hands.

How to play?

This digital theremin uses the same principles as a real one. The higher you go, and the louder the volume will be. The movements from left to right define the tone, from very low (left), to very high (right).

Why does it sound so weird?

In traditionnal music, each note is defined by a given frequency (e.g A = 440 Hz). However, the theremin doesn't have a neat seperation between notes, which means that to go from one note to another, you have to go through all the frequencies in between them. Those 'in-between notes' are very atypical to the human ear, which might give the feeling of hearing sounds from space

What is a waveform?

There are 4 types of waveforms : sine, square, sawtooth and triangle. The sine wave corresponds to a pure tone. The 3 others add harmonics to the original tone. The shape affects the way the note will sound like.

What are delay and reverb?

A delay is basically a repetition of the note you just played. The time between the original note and the repetion can vary to your liking. Reverb is a digital echo, giving the feeling that you are playing, for example, in a big hall.
